The Body Sculptor

by Eliezer Nussbaum

The Body Sculptor

The Body Sculptor depicts the saga of a plastic surgeon believed to have acquired HIV, the AIDS virus. Dr. Jason Stone, a renowned plastic surgeon, while rushing to catch a plane, finds out he’s HIV positive—a consequence of a near fatal incident during a humanitarian medical mission in the jungle.

Embroiled in his personal crisis, Stone misses the flight, which crashes right after takeoff. Unable to cope with what a grim future fate has dealt him, Stone allows everyone to believe he perished with the rest of the passengers—abandoning his wife and children, and his thriving practice.

Thus begins a dangerous odyssey, which entwines his path with that of an infamous drug lord. The Body Sculptor follows one man’s painful journey to find a new life . . . but the old one has not finished with him yet.
